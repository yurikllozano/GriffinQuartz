## 1. Executive Summary

### Where We Are Today

Griffin Quartz currently ranks for 414 organic keywords and receives roughly 31 organic visits per month, with a traffic cost value of $75/month. The domain rank (5,685,894) is extremely low. In practical terms, the site is nearly invisible in organic search.

However, there is a strong foundation to build on:

- **30 existing blog posts** covering cost, materials, and local topics
- **25+ location pages** across all of South Florida
- **13+ service pages** covering every major stone type and use case
- **33+ Cambria product pages** providing deep product content
- Several keywords already ranking on page 1 or page 2, including "cost of countertop installation in florida" (Position 5), "quartz countertops delray beach" (Position 5), and "l shaped countertop cost" (Position 8)

### The Problem

Despite having a lot of content, the site suffers from three critical SEO issues:

1. **Keyword Cannibalization** -- Four separate pages compete for "quartz countertops cost" and related pricing terms. Google does not know which page to rank, so none of them rank well (best position: 37).
2. **Geographic Imbalance** -- The blog is heavily tilted toward Fort Lauderdale, yet the company HQ is in Boca Raton and serves all of South Florida. Miami, Boca Raton, West Palm Beach, and broader "South Florida" content is thin.
3. **No Content Architecture** -- Posts exist as isolated pages with no pillar/cluster structure, weak internal linking, and no topical authority signals for Google.

### The Goal

By December 2026, this strategy aims to achieve:

- **2,500+ organic visits/month** (80x current traffic)
- **2,000+ organic keywords** (5x current)
- **Page 1 rankings** for 15+ high-value keywords
- **50+ monthly leads** (form fills + phone calls) from organic traffic

### The Approach

1. **Fix cannibalization immediately** -- Consolidate competing pages, set up redirects
2. **Build 6 pillar pages** -- Comprehensive, authoritative guides that anchor topic clusters
3. **Publish 22 new blog posts** (2/month, with 3 in some months) -- Each post targets specific keywords with KD under 35, supports a pillar page, and includes strong CTAs
4. **Implement strategic internal linking** -- Connect every post to its pillar, cross-link to service and location pages
5. **Build external authority** -- Local citations, guest posts, supplier partnerships

## 8. On-Page SEO Checklist for Every Blog Post

Use this checklist before publishing ANY new blog post or updating an existing one.

### Title Tag Formula

```
[Primary Keyword]: [Benefit or Descriptor] | Griffin Quartz
```

**Examples:**
- "Quartz vs Quartzite: What Is the Difference and Which Should You Choose? | Griffin Quartz"
- "Calacatta Quartz Countertops: Colors, Brands & Design Ideas | Griffin Quartz"
- "Kitchen Island Countertops: Materials, Overhangs & Waterfall Edges | Griffin Quartz"

**Rules:**
- Keep under 60 characters (Google truncates after that)
- Primary keyword as close to the start as possible
- Always end with "| Griffin Quartz" for brand recognition
- Do NOT keyword-stuff

### Meta Description Formula

```
[What the post covers in 1 sentence]. [Key benefit or unique angle]. [CTA: "Get a free quote" or "Call (720) 324-1436"].
```

**Examples:**
- "Compare quartz vs quartzite countertops: durability, cost, maintenance, and appearance. South Florida expert guide from Griffin Quartz. Get a free quote today."
- "Discover 15 stunning quartz and white cabinet combinations for your kitchen. Includes brand recommendations and South Florida design trends. Call (720) 324-1436."

**Rules:**
- Keep between 150-160 characters
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Include a call to action
- Make it compelling to click (this is your search listing ad copy)

### Heading Structure

```
H1: [Blog Post Title -- only ONE H1 per page]
  H2: [Major Section Heading -- include keywords naturally]
    H3: [Sub-section -- more specific detail]
    H3: [Sub-section]
  H2: [Next Major Section]
    H3: [Sub-section]
  H2: Frequently Asked Questions
    H3: [Question 1]
    H3: [Question 2]
```

**Rules:**
- Only 1 H1 tag per page (the article title)
- H2s for major sections (target 6-10 H2s per article)
- H3s for sub-sections under H2s
- Include target keywords in at least 2-3 H2s naturally
- Use question format for FAQ H3s (helps with featured snippets)
- Never skip heading levels (no H1 directly to H3)

### Image Optimization

For every image in every blog post:

1. **File name:** Descriptive, keyword-rich, hyphenated
   - Good: `calacatta-quartz-kitchen-island-boca-raton.webp`
   - Bad: `IMG_4532.jpg`
2. **Alt text:** Describe the image AND include a keyword naturally
   - Good: `alt="Calacatta quartz countertop on a kitchen island with waterfall edge in Boca Raton home"`
   - Bad: `alt="kitchen"` or `alt=""` (empty)
3. **File format:** WebP preferred, JPEG acceptable. No PNG for photos.
4. **File size:** Under 200KB per image. Compress with tools like ShortPixel or Squoosh.
5. **Dimensions:** Set explicit width and height attributes to prevent layout shift.
6. **Lazy loading:** Add `loading="lazy"` to all images except the hero/first image. First image should use `loading="eager"`.

### Schema Markup

Add structured data to every blog post. Three types to use:

**1. Article Schema (every post):**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Article",
  "headline": "[Post Title]",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Griffin Quartz"
  },
  "publisher": {
    "@type": "Organization",
    "name": "Griffin Quartz",
    "logo": {
      "@type": "ImageObject",
      "url": "https://griffinquartz.com/images/logo.png"
    }
  },
  "datePublished": "[YYYY-MM-DD]",
  "dateModified": "[YYYY-MM-DD]",
  "image": "[Hero image URL]",
  "description": "[Meta description]"
}
```

**2. FAQ Schema (posts with FAQ sections):**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "[Question text]",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "[Answer text]"
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

**3. HowTo Schema (step-by-step posts):**
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "HowTo",
  "name": "[How-to title]",
  "step": [
    {
      "@type": "HowToStep",
      "name": "[Step name]",
      "text": "[Step description]"
    }
  ]
}
```

### CTA Placement

Every blog post must have CTAs in these positions:

1. **After the introduction** (within first 300 words): Soft CTA, e.g., "Need help choosing? Get a free consultation."
2. **Mid-article** (after the most valuable section): Medium CTA, e.g., inline box with "Get a Free Quote" button linking to `/tools/quote-calculator`
3. **End of article** (after conclusion, before FAQ or after FAQ): Strong CTA with embedded form or prominent button. Include phone number (720) 324-1436.
4. **Sidebar** (desktop): Sticky CTA that follows the reader down the page with quote form or phone number.

### Additional On-Page Elements

- **Table of Contents:** Add a clickable TOC at the top of every post over 2,000 words
- **Estimated reading time:** Display at the top (e.g., "8 min read")
- **Author box:** Include at bottom with Griffin Quartz branding
