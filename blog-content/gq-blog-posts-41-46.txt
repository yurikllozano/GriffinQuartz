<h2>Why Quartz Is the Top Countertop Choice in Miami</h2>
<p>Miami's climate and lifestyle create specific demands that quartz meets better than almost any other countertop material. Here is why quartz dominates the Miami market:</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Humidity resistance:</strong> Miami averages over 70% relative humidity year-round. Natural stone countertops like marble and granite are porous and can absorb that ambient moisture, leading to discoloration, bacterial growth, and the need for frequent resealing. Quartz is engineered to be completely non-porous, so it shrugs off humidity without any maintenance.</li>
    <li><strong>Salt air durability:</strong> Homes in Miami Beach, Key Biscayne, and along the coast are constantly exposed to salt-laden air.     <li><strong>Stain resistance:</strong> Between the mangoes, the avocados, the turmeric, and the red wine, Miami kitchens see plenty of stain-causing ingredients. Quartz resists staining without any protective treatment.</li>
    <li><strong>Design versatility:</strong> Miami's architecture ranges from 1920s Art Deco to ultra-modern glass-and-steel construction. Quartz is available in hundreds of colors, patterns, and finishes, making it easy to match any design era or aesthetic preference.</li>
    <li><strong>Low maintenance:</strong> In a city where people would rather be at the beach than scrubbing countertops, the fact that quartz needs nothing more than a wipe with a damp cloth is a major selling point.</li>
